About Us

The Latin American Youth Center (LAYC) provides services that are geared towards fulfilling their mission of empowering a diverse population of youth. LAYC aims to assist youth to achieve a successful transition to adulthood, through multi-cultural, comprehensive, and innovative programs that address youths' social, academic, and career needs. LAYC values are that all youth are able to attain their dreams and goals, but also and acquire the skills and self-confidence to live a life of purpose, connection, contribution, and joy. LAYC was founded in 1968, with the goal of helping low-income families and their children. Since then, LAYC has expanded to offering multi-service, comprehensive, and bilingual enrichment, prevention, and intervention programs and opportunities in education, workforce readiness, housing, community building, mental health services, arts, and healthy recreation to over 4,000 individuals each year.

About “MI VIDA, MI VOZ”

Ana Ortez-Rivera proposed the program ““MI VIDA, MI VOZ” to empower Hispanic youth to combat anti-immigrant stigmas through expressing their narratives with art. Ten participants were selected and worked in groups of two in order to create mix-media art projects. The program was run by LAYC from mid January through mid March. Participants were guided by mentors from the University of Maryland, College Park.

To find out more information, or to enroll in the next session of MI VIDA, MI VOZ - visit http://www.layc-dc.org.
